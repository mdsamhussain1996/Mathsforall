① Foundation: Geometry & Math

What exactly are eigenvalues geometrically?

A linear transformation usually knocks vectors off their original span. But for certain special vectors, the transformation only stretches or flips them. These are the eigenvectors, and the scaling factor is the eigenvalue (\(\lambda\)).

\[A\mathbf{v} = \lambda\mathbf{v}\]

To find them algebraically, we solve the characteristic equation:

\[\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\]
  • Trace: Sum of eigenvalues = Sum of diagonal elements.
  • Determinant: Product of eigenvalues = \(\det(A)\).
  • Symmetric Matrices: Always have real, orthogonal eigenvectors.

② Principal Component Analysis (PCA)

Finding the axes of maximum variance

From Covariance to Variance

PCA computes the covariance matrix of our data, then finds its eigenvectors. These vectors (Principal Components) point in the directions of maximum variance. The corresponding eigenvalues tell us exactly how much variance that component captures.

③ SVD & Image Compression

Eckart-Young Theorem in Action

Singular Value Decomposition factorizes any matrix \(A = U \Sigma V^T\). The singular values in \(\Sigma\) are the square roots of the eigenvalues of \(A^T A\). By keeping only the top \(k\) singular values, we create the optimal rank-\(k\) approximation of the image.

⑤ Recommender Systems

Latent Factor Analysis (Netflix Prize)

If we have a huge sparse matrix of Users \(\times\) Movies, SVD factors it into User Latent Vectors and Movie Latent Vectors. The dot product of these vectors predicts missing ratings.

This means users and movies are mapped into the same geometric space spanned by the eigenvectors of the ratings matrix.

⑥ Spectral Clustering & PageRank

Eigenvectors of Graphs and Networks

Spectral Graph Partitioning

By computing the eigenvectors of the Graph Laplacian matrix, the second smallest eigenvector (the Fiedler Vector) naturally assigns values to nodes that split the graph into two optimal clusters, severing the weakest links.

Colors assigned by Fiedler Vector value

Google's PageRank

PageRank is literally just finding the dominant eigenvector of the Web's adjacency/stochastic matrix. The Power Iteration algorithm repeatedly multiplies a vector by the matrix until it converges to this eigenvector.
