Mathematics for Physics

PDEs, Fourier analysis, tensors, and complex analysis

Partial Differential Equations

Physical laws are expressed as PDEs. The heat equation describes diffusion. The wave equation governs sound and light. Schrödinger's equation is the PDE of quantum mechanics. Mastering PDEs is essential for theoretical physics.

Heat: ∂u/∂t = α∇²u Wave: ∂²u/∂t² = c²∇²u Laplace: ∇²φ = 0 Schrödinger: iℏ∂ψ/∂t = Ĥψ

Fourier Analysis

Any periodic function can be decomposed into sines and cosines. The Fourier transform extends this to aperiodic signals. It converts problems in the time/space domain to frequency domain — often making intractable equations solvable.

f(x) = Σ [aₙcos(nπx/L) + bₙsin(nπx/L)] F(ω) = ∫ f(t) e^{-iωt} dt

  • Fourier series & transforms
  • PDEs: heat, wave, Laplace
  • Tensor calculus & general relativity
  • Complex analysis & conformal maps
  • Group theory in quantum mechanics

Mathematics for Economics

Game theory, optimization, stochastic processes, and dynamic programming

Calculus in Economics

Marginal analysis — derivatives — is the core tool of microeconomics. The profit-maximizing firm sets marginal revenue equal to marginal cost. Consumer surplus, elasticity, and optimization are all calculus in disguise.

max π(q) = R(q) − C(q) FOC: R'(q) = C'(q) → MR = MC

Game Theory

How do rational agents make decisions when outcomes depend on others' choices? Nash equilibrium — where no player can unilaterally improve their outcome — is a fixed point of a best-response mapping. This is pure mathematics!

Nash Equilibrium: uᵢ(sᵢ*, s₋ᵢ*) ≥ uᵢ(sᵢ, s₋ᵢ*) ∀sᵢ ∈ Sᵢ, ∀i

Stochastic Processes

Financial markets are modeled as stochastic differential equations. The Black-Scholes option pricing formula — worth a Nobel Prize — comes from solving a specific PDE derived from a geometric Brownian motion model.

dS = μS dt + σS dW (geometric Brownian motion) Black-Scholes PDE: ∂V/∂t + ½σ²S²∂²V/∂S² + rS∂V/∂S = rV

Mathematics for Data Science

Statistics, information theory, dimensionality reduction

Statistical Inference

Data science is applied statistics. Hypothesis testing, confidence intervals, p-values — these are rigorous mathematical frameworks for drawing conclusions from data while quantifying uncertainty.

Confidence Interval: x̄ ± z_{α/2} · σ/√n t-statistic: t = (x̄ − μ₀) / (s/√n)

Information Theory

Shannon entropy measures information content. Mutual information measures statistical dependence. KL divergence measures "distance" between distributions. These concepts underlie compression, communication, and modern ML.

H(X) = −Σ p(x) log p(x) (entropy) I(X;Y) = H(X) − H(X|Y) (mutual info) KL(P||Q) = Σ P(x) log(P(x)/Q(x))

Dimensionality Reduction

High-dimensional data is hard to visualize and analyze. PCA (linear) and t-SNE / UMAP (nonlinear) project data to lower dimensions while preserving structure. Both rely on linear algebra and optimization.

  • Descriptive & inferential statistics
  • Regression (linear, logistic)
  • Entropy & information theory
  • PCA, t-SNE, UMAP
  • Clustering (k-means, GMMs)
  • Bayesian inference & MCMC

Mathematics for Biology

Population dynamics, reaction kinetics, and epidemiological models

Population Dynamics

The logistic growth model captures how a population grows rapidly when small but slows as it approaches carrying capacity K. The Lotka-Volterra equations model predator-prey oscillations — a beautiful nonlinear system.

Logistic: dN/dt = rN(1 − N/K) Predator-prey: dN/dt = αN − βNP dP/dt = δNP − γP

Epidemiological Models

The SIR model divides a population into Susceptible, Infected, and Recovered compartments. Differential equations govern the transitions. The basic reproduction number R₀ determines whether an epidemic spreads.

dS/dt = -βSI/N dI/dt = βSI/N − γI dR/dt = γI R₀ = β/γ

Reaction-Diffusion Systems

Turing's 1952 paper showed how reaction-diffusion PDEs can spontaneously generate spatial patterns — explaining animal coat patterns, zebra stripes, and leopard spots through pure mathematics!
