Euler's Identity
eiπ + 1 = 0
Considered the most beautiful equation in mathematics, it elegantly unites five fundamental constants: e (Euler's number), i (imaginary unit), π (pi), 1 (multiplicative identity), and 0 (additive identity). It arises from Euler's formula eiθ = cos θ + i sin θ evaluated at θ = π.

Vectors, Matrices & Transformations

Linear algebra is the language of data. Every image, audio clip, or text document is a vector. Neural networks are compositions of linear transformations with non-linear activations.

💡 Intuition: A matrix is a machine that takes a vector and outputs a transformed vector — stretching, rotating, projecting it into a new space.
A·x = λx  (eigenvalue equation)
A = UΣVT  (SVD decomposition)

Probability & Statistics

Probability provides a rigorous framework for reasoning under uncertainty. Bayesian methods allow us to update beliefs with new evidence — the foundation of modern ML.

💡 Intuition: Bayes' theorem tells you how to update your beliefs when you see new data. Prior knowledge + evidence = posterior belief.
P(A|B) = P(B|A)·P(A) / P(B)  (Bayes)
E[X] = Σ x·P(X=x)
N(μ,σ²): f(x) = (1/σ√2π)·e^(-(x-μ)²/2σ²)

Optimization & Gradient Descent

Training a neural network is an optimization problem: minimize a loss function over millions of parameters. Gradient descent is the engine of modern AI.

💡 Intuition: Imagine rolling a ball down a hilly landscape. Gradient descent follows the steepest slope downward, step by step, until reaching a valley (minimum).
θ ← θ - α·∇L(θ)
∇L(θ) = ∂L/∂θ  (gradient)
Adam: m_t = β₁m_{t-1} + (1-β₁)g_t

Forward & Inverse Kinematics

Kinematics studies the geometry of motion without considering forces. Given joint angles, where is the end-effector? (forward) — or given a target position, what joint angles are needed? (inverse)

💡 Intuition: Forward kinematics is like computing where your finger is given your arm posture. Inverse kinematics is figuring out how to bend your arm to touch a specific point.
FK: p = T₁(θ₁)·T₂(θ₂)·...·Tₙ(θₙ)·p₀
DH: T = Rot_z(θ)·Trans_z(d)·Trans_x(a)·Rot_x(α)
IK: θ = J⁺(q)·Δp  (Jacobian pseudoinverse)

Coordinate Transformations

Robots operate in 3D space and must transform coordinates between frames: world, robot base, tool. Homogeneous matrices encode both rotation and translation in a single 4×4 matrix.

💡 Intuition: A rotation matrix spins the coordinate axes. A translation shifts the origin. Combined in homogeneous form, they describe any rigid body motion.
R_z(θ) = [[cos θ, -sin θ, 0],[sin θ, cos θ, 0],[0, 0, 1]]
T = [[R, t],[0 0 0, 1]]  (4×4 homogeneous)
p_world = T_base^world · p_base

Differential Equations in Robotics

Robot dynamics are governed by differential equations relating forces, torques, inertia, and motion. The Newton-Euler and Lagrangian formulations are the two main approaches.

💡 Intuition: F=ma is a differential equation (a = ẍ). For robots with many joints, this becomes a system of coupled ODEs that must be solved in real time.
M(q)q̈ + C(q,q̇)q̇ + G(q) = τ
d/dt(∂L/∂q̇ᵢ) - ∂L/∂qᵢ = τᵢ  (Lagrange)
ẋ = f(x,u)  (state-space ODE)

Dynamical Systems

A dynamical system describes how a state evolves over time according to a rule (differential or difference equation). The state captures all information needed to predict future behavior.

💡 Intuition: The state of a pendulum is its angle and angular velocity. Given these two numbers, the entire future motion is determined (for a deterministic system).
ẋ(t) = f(x(t), u(t))  (continuous)
x[k+1] = f(x[k], u[k])  (discrete)
Linear: ẋ = Ax + Bu, y = Cx + Du
EquilibriaPhase portraitsBifurcations ChaosLimit cycles

Applications: Climate models, Population dynamics, Electrical circuits, Epidemiology

Lyapunov Stability Analysis

Lyapunov's method proves stability without solving the differential equation. A Lyapunov function is an energy-like function that decreases along trajectories — if it exists, the system is stable.

💡 Intuition: Think of a ball in a bowl. If we can show that the system's "energy" always decreases (like friction dissipating kinetic energy), the ball will settle at the bottom — the equilibrium.
V(x) > 0, V(0) = 0  (positive definite)
V̇(x) = ∇V · f(x) ≤ 0  (stability)
V̇(x) < 0  (asymptotic stability)
LaSalle's principleLyapunov function BIBO stabilityHurwitz matrix

Applications: Flight control, Power grids, Chemical reactors, Autonomous vehicles

Controllability & Observability

Can we drive the system to any state? (controllability) Can we infer the full state from outputs? (observability) These properties determine whether a control design is even possible.

💡 Intuition: A car is controllable — you can reach any location from any starting point. If the speedometer is broken, you might lack observability of the full vehicle state.
𝒞 = [B, AB, A²B, ..., Aⁿ⁻¹B]  (controllability)
rank(𝒞) = n ↔ fully controllable
𝒪 = [C; CA; CA²; ...; CAⁿ⁻¹]  (observability)
Pole placementLQRKalman filter Observer designMinimal realization

Applications: Rocket guidance, Autonomous navigation, Multi-agent systems, Switched systems
